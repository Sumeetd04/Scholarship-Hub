📋 Registration & OTR

OTR (One Time Registration) is a unique reference number allotted to every student on the National Scholarship Portal. It is valid for your entire academic career — you only need to register once. From AY 2024-25, OTR is mandatory to apply for any scholarship on NSP. You can generate it using your Aadhaar number or Aadhaar Enrollment ID (EID).

Visit scholarships.gov.in and click on "New Registration". Enter your Aadhaar number or Enrollment ID, verify via OTP sent to your Aadhaar-linked mobile number, fill in your basic details, and your OTR will be generated instantly. You can also use the NSP OTR App available on Google Play Store.

Aadhaar is mandatory for OTR generation. However, if you do not have Aadhaar, you can use your Aadhaar Enrollment ID (EID) to register temporarily. You must complete Aadhaar authentication within a specified period to continue receiving scholarship benefits. Contact your district welfare office for assistance.

On the NSP login page, click "Forgot Password". Enter your registered mobile number or OTR/Application ID. An OTP will be sent to your Aadhaar-linked mobile number. Enter the OTP to verify, then set a new password. If your mobile number has changed, contact the NSP helpdesk at 0120-6619540.

📝 Application Process

Scholarship applications for AY 2024-25 are currently open on the National Scholarship Portal. Fresh applications typically open in September–October and close in November–December. Renewal applications follow a similar timeline. Always check the Announcements section for exact dates as they vary by scheme and state.

As per NSP guidelines, a student can apply for only one scholarship per academic year through the portal to avoid duplication. However, some private and state scholarships run independently — you may apply for those separately. Please read the eligibility criteria of each scheme carefully before applying.

A Fresh application is for students who are applying for the scholarship for the first time. A Renewal application is for students who received the scholarship in the previous academic year and wish to continue receiving it. Renewal applications require uploading the previous year's result/marksheet and confirming continued eligibility.

College verification is mandatory for your application to proceed. Visit your college's accounts or scholarship department and request them to verify your application on NSP. Colleges are required to verify within 30 days of the portal opening. If verification is delayed, escalate to your district welfare or nodal officer.

📄 Documents Required

The standard documents required are: Aadhaar card, caste/category certificate (SC/ST/OBC), income certificate (issued by a gazetted officer), previous year marksheet, bonafide/admission letter from college, bank account details (in the student's name), and passport-size photograph. Some schemes may require additional documents — check the scheme-specific guidelines.

Income limits vary by scheme. For most central government post-matric scholarships (SC/ST/OBC), the annual family income limit is ₹2.5 lakh. For minority scholarships, it is ₹1 lakh. The Central Sector Scheme for merit students has a limit of ₹8 lakh. Always check the specific scheme's eligibility before applying.

Yes, caste certificates issued in regional/state languages are accepted. However, it is advisable to also have a certified English translation, especially if you are applying for scholarships in a different state. The certificate must be issued by a competent authority (Tahsildar or above) and should be current/valid.

💰 Payment & Disbursal

After your application is sanctioned by the district officer, the amount is typically credited to your bank account within 7–30 working days via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). The timeline depends on the scheme, state, and banking processing time. You can track your payment status using your Application ID on this portal.

First, check your application status on NSP to confirm it shows "Sanctioned" or "Payment Transferred". If yes, verify that your bank account is Aadhaar-linked and NPCI-mapped. Contact your bank if the amount hasn't arrived within 10 working days of the payment date. You can also raise a grievance on the NSP portal or call the helpdesk at 0120-6619540.

No, scholarship amounts must be credited to a bank account held solely in the student's name. Joint accounts are not accepted. If you are a minor, a guardian's account may be accepted in some schemes — check the specific scheme guidelines. Ensure your account is active, linked to Aadhaar, and NPCI-mapped for DBT.

💻 Technical Issues

Try the following steps: clear your browser cache and cookies, use Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox (recommended browsers), disable browser extensions, and try again. Ensure all mandatory fields are filled and documents are under the specified file size (usually 200 KB per document). If the issue persists, call the NSP helpdesk at 0120-6619540 or email [email protected].

Ensure your mobile number is the same one linked to your Aadhaar. Check your signal strength and that your number is active. OTPs expire in 10 minutes — request a new one if it has expired. If your Aadhaar-linked mobile number has changed, visit the nearest Aadhaar Seva Kendra (CSC) to update it before applying.

Log in to your NSP account and go to your profile/application section. Bank account details can be updated before the application is forwarded by your college. Once forwarded, you must contact your college or district officer to make corrections. Always double-check your IFSC code and account number before saving.
